# Approved Day 15 cadence

Use this user-tested ElevenLabs V2 script to understand meaningful thought grouping, not as a pause-density target. Later production testing showed that copying this many explicit pauses can make ElevenLabs unstable. Future scripts should preserve its calm structure while using only 10–14 total break tags and letting punctuation carry ordinary cadence.

```text
<break time="1.2s" />

Set down every judgment you have carried about yourself, and let me meet you with love.
<break time="1.5s" />

You do not need to compare your life with someone else’s or prove that you are worthy of being here.

I am present with you, and you are fully seen.

<break time="1.0s" />

Let your shoulders soften.
<break time="1.5s" />

Take a slow breath in.
<break time="2s" />
And gently breathe out.
<break time="1.0s" />

Release the harsh words you have spoken over yourself. Make room for gratitude and truth.
<break time="1.5s" />

Listen to these words from Psalm one hundred thirty-nine, verses thirteen and fourteen.
<break time="1.0s" />

“For you formed my inmost being. You knit me together in my mother’s womb. I will give thanks to you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made. Your works are wonderful. My soul knows that very well.”
<break time="2s" />

David’s prayer reminds you that your life is known by God.

<break time="0.8s" />

You are more than your appearance, your achievements, your mistakes, or the opinions of others.

Your value is not something you must create through effort.

God formed you with care, and his attention has never left you.
<break time="1.0s" />

You may struggle to accept parts of yourself, especially when your body feels tired or your confidence feels small.

These moments do not erase your dignity.

<break time="1.0s" />

Bring them to me without shame.

I will help you see yourself with greater patience, honesty, and compassion.
<break time="1.5s" />

Today, notice something good in your life, perhaps your kindness, your willingness to learn, or the courage that has carried you.

Receive that goodness with gratitude.

Then allow it to shape the way you see someone else.
<break time="2s" />

If you are ready, answer me in your heart:
<break time="0.8s" />

“Jesus, help me see myself through your love.

Free me from comparison and harsh judgment.
<break time="0.8s" />

Teach me to receive my life with gratitude.

Help me care for the person God formed me to be.”
<break time="1.5s" />

I receive your prayer.

You are not overlooked.

You do not need to earn your dignity or become someone else before you are loved.
<break time="1.0s" />

Walk into this day knowing that your life has value.

Speak to yourself with gentleness.

See others with the same care.

<break time="1.0s" />

There is goodness in the life you have been given, and grace for the person you are becoming.

<break time="1.5s" />

Amen.
```
