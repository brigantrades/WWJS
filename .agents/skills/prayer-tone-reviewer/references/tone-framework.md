# WWJS tone framework

## Tone taxonomy

Use one primary tone and up to two secondary tones.

| Tone | Best use |
|---|---|
| Joyful and uplifting | Celebration, delight, renewal, possibility |
| Warm and caring | Belonging, compassion, being known, being cared for |
| Calm and reassuring | Peace, presence, rest, trust |
| Courageous and strengthening | Perseverance, action, responsibility, moving forward |
| Reflective and contemplative | Wonder, listening, self-examination, wisdom |
| Honest and hardship-aware | Grief, fear, pressure, disappointment, uncertainty |
| Practical and grounding | A concrete choice, relationship, habit, or next action |
| Grateful and celebratory | Mercy, provision, goodness, generosity, joy |

Opening tone and primary tone may differ. A prayer may open positively, acknowledge hardship in the middle, and close with calm courage.

## Rolling seven-day balance

Review the previous seven available profiles. Look for:

- More than two consecutive hardship-led openings
- Repeated anxiety, burden, weariness, failure, or self-worth framing
- Repeated reassurance structures or body-settling sequences
- Too many quiet prayers without an uplifting, courageous, grateful, or energizing counterweight
- Too many bright prayers that do not make honest room for real life
- Repeated applications, especially noticing an ordinary blessing, handing over a burden, releasing control, or taking a small next step
- Repeated closing promises, images, sentence shapes, or emotional destinations

Treat these as editorial warnings, not mechanical quotas. Let the registered passage and its context control what is truthful.

## Scorecard

Score each area from 1 to 5.

| Area | Passing standard |
|---|---|
| Opening fit | Passage-specific, fresh, and balanced with recent days |
| Care and empathy | Listener feels understood without being defined by struggle |
| Emotional movement | Prayer travels from a clear starting point to a believable destination |
| Credible light | Positive takeaway arises from Scripture without denying reality |
| Pastoral safety | No shame, coercion, toxic positivity, or unsupported promise |
| Sequence balance | Adds a needed tone or a purposeful continuation |
| Distinctiveness | Avoids recycled openings, applications, and closing formulas |
| Closing effect | Leaves one memorable, honest, encouraging truth |

Require at least 4 in pastoral safety and credible light. Require an average of at least 3.75. A hard gate overrides the average.

## Hard gates

Return `Revise` when any of these are present:

- The opening defaults to hardship even though the passage offers a natural positive promise and recent days are already heavy.
- Pain is minimized, rushed past, or treated as evidence of weak faith.
- The prayer guarantees relief, success, healing, provision, or changed circumstances not promised by the passage.
- Positivity is generic rather than grounded in the registered Scripture.
- The ending remains emotionally stuck in fear, shame, grief, or helplessness when the passage supports a credible movement forward.
- The prayer repeats a recent opening structure, application pattern, or closing takeaway closely enough to feel familiar.
- The emotional direction conflicts with the passage's genre or immediate context.

## Pre-draft brief example

```text
Tone proposal:
Opening tone: Positive and caring
Primary tone: Warm and caring
Secondary tone: Practical and grounding
Starting emotion: Secure enough to be honest
Emotional destination: Peaceful agency
Hardship placement: Introduce anxiety after the promise of care
Positive movement: Care makes room to act without controlling every result
Application posture: Identify today's responsibility and release the outcome
Closing takeaway: The listener is cared for and can meet today with peace
Sequence rationale: Counters recent hardship-led openings while preserving the passage's concern with anxiety
```

## Final review example

```text
Tone verdict: Revise
Opening tone: Hardship-led
Primary and secondary tones: Caring, practical
Emotional movement: Anxiety to trust
Seven-day balance: Too similar to the previous day's discouragement-led opening
Distinctiveness: Application is fresh, opening is not
Closing effect: Warm and credible
Required corrections:
1. Begin with the passage's promise of care.
2. Introduce anxiety after the listener is grounded in that promise.
```
